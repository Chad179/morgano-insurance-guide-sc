South Carolina Insurance Requirements

South Carolina mandates specific minimum insurance coverages that every resident should understand. These are legal floors, not recommended coverage levels. Most Greenville insurance professionals advise carrying limits well above the state minimums.

Auto Insurance (Mandatory)

  • Bodily Injury: $25,000 per person / $50,000 per accident
  • Property Damage: $25,000 per accident
  • Uninsured Motorist: 25/50/25 (can reject in writing)
  • Written as 25/50/25 liability limits
  • Proof of insurance required at registration

Workers Compensation (Business)

  • Required for businesses with 4+ employees per SC Code 42-1-360
  • Construction: required with 1+ employees
  • Covers medical, lost wages, rehab, death benefits
  • Premiums based on class code, payroll, and EMR
  • Annual premium audit verifies payroll

Homeowners insurance is not legally mandated by South Carolina, but your mortgage lender will require it as a condition of the loan. If your property is in a FEMA-designated flood zone with a federally backed mortgage, flood insurance through the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) or a private flood carrier is also required.

Auto Insurance in Greenville

Greenville drivers face specific risk factors that affect auto insurance premiums. The I-85 corridor carries more than 100,000 vehicles per day through the metro area. The I-385 interchange connecting Simpsonville, Mauldin, and downtown Greenville is a frequent accident location. Woodruff Road between I-385 and Verdae is one of the most congested retail corridors in the Upstate, with constant fender-benders during peak hours.

Coverage Types Explained

  • Liability (BI/PD): Pays for injuries and damage you cause to others. The 25/50/25 state minimum is often inadequate for serious accidents. Many Greenville agents recommend 100/300/100.
  • Collision: Pays to repair your car after an accident regardless of fault. Required by lenders on financed vehicles.
  • Comprehensive: Covers non-collision damage: hail, theft, deer strikes, vandalism, flooding, falling objects. Essential in hail-prone Greenville County.
  • Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UM/UIM): Protects you when the at-fault driver has no insurance or not enough. South Carolina has one of the highest uninsured driver rates in the nation.
  • Medical Payments (MedPay): Covers medical expenses for you and your passengers regardless of fault, up to the policy limit.
  • Gap Insurance: Covers the difference between your loan balance and the car's actual cash value if totaled. Critical for new vehicles with low down payments.

SR-22 Filing

Drivers who have had their SC license suspended for DUI, excessive points, or driving without insurance need an SR-22 certificate filed with the SC DMV before reinstatement. The SR-22 is an electronic certification from your insurer confirming you carry at least minimum liability limits. The requirement typically lasts three years. Not all carriers write SR-22 policies, but an independent agency like The Morgano Agency shops multiple high-risk carriers to find competitive rates.

Homeowners and Renters Insurance

A standard HO-3 homeowners policy covers your dwelling on an open-perils basis (everything is covered unless specifically excluded) and your personal property on a named-perils basis. The HO-5 extends open-perils coverage to personal property as well, providing broader protection at a higher premium.

Key components of your homeowners policy include:

  • Dwelling coverage (Coverage A): Pays to rebuild your home. Should reflect current reconstruction costs, not market value.
  • Other structures (Coverage B): Covers detached garages, sheds, fences. Typically 10% of dwelling limit.
  • Personal property (Coverage C): Covers belongings inside the home. Choose between replacement cost and actual cash value (ACV) settlement.
  • Loss of use (Coverage D): Pays living expenses if your home is uninhabitable after a covered loss.
  • Liability (Coverage E): Protects you if someone is injured on your property or you damage someone else's property.
  • Medical payments (Coverage F): Covers minor injuries to guests regardless of fault.

Renters Insurance (HO-4)

Renters in Greenville apartments and rental homes need an HO-4 policy. It covers your personal property against named perils, provides liability coverage, and includes loss of use if you are displaced. The landlord's policy covers the building structure but NOT your belongings inside it. Many Greenville apartment complexes along Pelham Road, Woodruff Road, and in the Wade Hampton corridor now require proof of renters insurance before signing a lease.

Flood Insurance and Local Risk Factors

Standard homeowners and renters policies do NOT cover flood damage. Flood insurance is a separate policy. If your property is near the Reedy River, Saluda River, or their tributaries, you may be in a FEMA flood zone that requires flood coverage.

Greenville County has multiple flood-prone areas. Properties along the Reedy River corridor from Cleveland Park through Conestee, near the Saluda River in the northwestern part of the county, and in lower-elevation neighborhoods along tributaries have experienced significant flooding during heavy rain events. The August 2020 storms and other historic flood events demonstrate that flood risk extends well beyond FEMA-mapped high-risk zones.

Flood insurance is available through the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP), administered by FEMA, and through private flood insurers. NFIP policies have maximum limits of $250,000 for dwelling and $100,000 for contents on residential properties. If your home's value exceeds those limits, a private flood policy or an NFIP excess flood policy fills the gap. NFIP policies carry a 30-day waiting period after purchase, so buying during or after a flood warning is too late.

Business Insurance Essentials

Greenville's economy spans manufacturing along the I-85 corridor (anchored by BMW and Michelin), healthcare (Prisma Health, Bon Secours St. Francis), technology startups downtown, restaurants and retail on Main Street and Woodruff Road, construction in booming development areas like West Greenville and Verdae, and professional services throughout the county. Each industry has specific insurance requirements.

Core Business Coverages

  • Commercial General Liability (CGL): Covers third-party bodily injury, property damage, and advertising injury. Standard limits are $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate limit. Written on either an occurrence or claims-made basis.
  • Workers Compensation: Mandatory for SC businesses with 4+ employees (1+ in construction). Covers employee injuries, medical costs, and lost wages. Premiums determined by class code, payroll, and experience modification rate. Subject to annual premium audit.
  • Business Owners Policy (BOP): Bundles commercial property, general liability, and business interruption coverage at a discount. Ideal for small to mid-size Greenville businesses.
  • Commercial Auto: Covers business-owned vehicles. Personal auto policies exclude business use.
  • Professional Liability (E&O): Covers errors, omissions, and negligence in professional services. Essential for consultants, accountants, architects, IT firms, and healthcare providers.
  • Cyber Liability: Covers data breach costs, forensic investigation, notification expenses, business interruption from cyber events, and ransomware/cyber extortion. Critical for any business that stores customer data electronically.

Certificate of Insurance (COI): Greenville general contractors, commercial landlords, and government entities routinely require COIs from vendors and subcontractors. The declarations page of your policy lists your coverages, limits, and deductibles. A COI confirms to third parties that your coverage is in force. Life Insurance Overview

Life insurance provides a death benefit to your beneficiaries. The two primary categories are term life and permanent life insurance.

  • Term life: Covers a specific period (10, 20, or 30 years). If you die during the term, the policy pays the death benefit. If you outlive the term, coverage ends. Term life is significantly less expensive than permanent life and is appropriate for most Greenville families who need coverage during their working years, while children are at home, or while a mortgage is outstanding.
  • Whole life: Covers your entire life as long as premiums are paid. Builds cash value over time that you can borrow against. Higher premiums than term life.
  • Universal life: Permanent coverage with flexible premiums and death benefits. Cash value grows based on current interest rates or market performance (in variable and indexed UL products).

Business owners also use life insurance for key person coverage (protecting the business if a critical employee dies), buy-sell agreement funding (ensuring business partners can buy out a deceased partner's share), and executive benefits.

Severe Weather

The Upstate experiences severe thunderstorms with damaging hail from March through June. Hailstones can dent vehicles, strip roof shingles, crack siding, and shatter skylights. Wind events from thunderstorms and occasional tornadoes cause tree damage and structural damage. Many carriers apply separate wind/hail deductibles (often 1-2% of the dwelling limit) that are significantly higher than the standard all-peril deductible.

Traffic and Accidents

The convergence of I-85 and I-385 in Greenville creates high accident frequency. Woodruff Road, Pelham Road, Wade Hampton Boulevard, Pleasantburg Drive, and Augusta Road all carry heavy daily traffic. Higher accident rates in urban ZIP codes (29601, 29607, 29615) translate to higher auto premiums compared to rural Greenville County addresses.

Flood Risk

While Greenville is not on the coast, inland flooding from heavy rain events affects properties near the Reedy River, Saluda River, and their tributaries. Flash flooding during intense thunderstorms can affect areas well outside FEMA-mapped flood zones. Neighborhoods in Conestee, along the Reedy River corridor through downtown and Cleveland Park, and near creek tributaries in Overbrook and Augusta Road areas have flood exposure.
